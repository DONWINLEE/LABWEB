About

Yi Yaolin

Associate Professor
School of Civil and Environmental Engineering
Nanyang Technological University
Email DR-NTU Scholar

Dr. Yi Yaolin is an Associate Professor in the School of Civil and Environmental Engineering at Nanyang Technological University (NTU), Singapore. He was the Deputy Co-Director of the Surbana Jurong–NTU Corporate Laboratory (2022–2024), a $61 million initiative developing next-generation sustainable solutions for complex urban and industrial challenges.

Dr. Yi's research centres on improving sustainability in geotechnical engineering through interdisciplinary approaches that bridge geotechnical engineering, materials engineering, and environmental engineering. His group focuses on sustainable geo-materials, geo-environmental engineering, and advanced ground improvement technologies.

He serves as Associate Editor for the Journal of Materials in Civil Engineering (ASCE), and as Editorial Board Member for Geotechnical Engineering (ICE) and Ground Improvement (ICE). He is Secretary of the ISSMGE Technical Committee on Sustainability in Geotechnical Engineering (TC307), a member of the ASCE Geo-Institute Soil Improvement Committee, and a member of the Singapore Standards Council Technical Committee on Civil and Geotechnical Work.

Research

Research Areas

Sustainable Geotechnology

Eco-efficient ground improvement using industrial by-products and wastes, reducing carbon footprint in geotechnical and foundation engineering.

Hazardous Waste Treatment

Treatment and valorisation of hazardous industrial wastes including contaminated soils, heavy-metal-bearing sludges, and mine tailings.

Coastal Protection Materials

Durable, corrosion-resistant materials for coastal and marine geotechnical applications to address sea-level rise and coastal infrastructure challenges.

Carbonation & CO₂ Sequestration

Harnessing carbonation reactions for carbon capture, strength enhancement, and hazard mitigation in reactive construction materials.

Deep Soil Mixing

Innovative binders and mixing techniques for soft ground improvement and low-permeability cut-off wall construction in urban and coastal settings.

Geo-Environmental Engineering

Interdisciplinary research linking geotechnical, environmental, and materials engineering for contaminated land, landfill, and marine environments.
