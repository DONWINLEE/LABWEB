Research

Yi Group@NTU attempts to use interdisciplinary approaches to improve the sustainability in geotechnical engineering. Specifically, Yi Group@NTU is working in interdisciplinary areas among geotechnical engineering, materials engineering, and environmental engineering. Research topics in Dr. Yi's group include:

  • Treatment of problematical soils (e.g. soft soils, sufate-bearing soils & contaminated soils) using novel materials/methods
  • Efficient rock excavation and concrete demolition using sustainable soundless cracking agents
  • Coastal underground seawater intrusion prevention and water resource utilization
  • Durable construction materials for coastal protection and marine geotechnology
  • Treatment and utilization of industry by-products/wastes (e.g. IBA, IFA, steel slag, carbide sludge & freshwater sludge)
  • Carbonation of alkaline materials/wastes for CO2 sequestration, hazard mitigation & strength enhancement

Representative projects

  • Creating underground reservoirs and protecting reclaimed lands using seepage cut-off walls: one stone, two birds. Academic Research Fund Tier 2, Ministry of Education, 2026-2029, PI
  • Development and field demonstration of low-cost durable seepage cut-off walls for preventing seawater intrusion based on sustainably sourced materials. Public Utilities Board (PUB), 2025-2028, PI
  • Sustainable soil stabilization using novel binders for various geotechnical applications in Singapore. Building and Construction Authority (BCA), EnGro Corporation Limited, and Zhao Yang Geotechnic Pte Ltd, 2024-2026, PI
  • Treating excavated soft marine clay as filling materials. NTUitive Pte Ltd, 2024-2026, PI
  • Utilization of freshwater sludge and carbide sludge to produce expansive agent for civil engineering applications. National Research Foundation (NRF), National Environment Agency (NEA), 2021-2025, PI
  • A sustainable and economical binder for construction of low permeable and high durable seepage cut-off wall in marine environment: preparing for sea-level rise in Singapore. Academic Research Fund Tier 2, Ministry of Education (MOE), 2021-2025, PI
  • Resource recovery and treatment of incineration fly ash of municipal solid waste. Academic Research Fund Tier 1, Ministry of Education (MOE), 2021-2024, PI
